Designing Conversion-Ready Pages with AI

What separates a good AI-generated site from a client-ready one — and how to prompt for the difference.

ApexSites Team·10 min read

Any AI website generator can produce a page with a hero, some text, and a button. The gap between that and a page that actually converts visitors into calls or bookings comes down to a handful of decisions — most of which come from how you prompt and refine the build, not the tool itself.

Start with the one action you want

Before generating anything, decide the single primary action the page should drive: book a call, request a quote, call a phone number. Every generated site should have exactly one primary call to action repeated at the top and bottom of the page — not three competing buttons fighting for attention.

Specificity beats generic AI-generated copy

The fastest way to make a site feel like an unedited AI output is generic copy: "we provide quality services you can trust." Prompt for and edit toward specifics — years in business, a real service area, a concrete guarantee, an actual review count. A standard build includes one quality pass; use it to replace anything that reads as a placeholder with a real, specific claim.

Proof elements clients actually respond to

  • A star rating and review count near the primary CTA
  • Licensing, insurance, or certification badges for trades and professional services
  • A short list of recognizable service-area towns or neighborhoods
  • A named guarantee ("50-year workmanship warranty," "same-week scheduling") rather than a vague promise

When to use a revision vs. a full regeneration

A simple copy or design tweak — swapping a headline, adjusting a color, changing a CTA label — is a lightweight, low-cost revision. Reserve a full design regeneration for cases where the entire layout or direction is wrong, since it's a heavier action. Knowing the difference keeps your AXX Credit usage efficient across a full month of client builds.

A pre-delivery checklist

  • One clear primary CTA, repeated at top and bottom
  • Real business specifics — no placeholder text remaining
  • At least one trust or proof element above the fold
  • Mobile layout checked, not just desktop
