Turning Websites into Recurring Hosting Revenue

How to price, position, and package hosting as a monthly service clients keep paying for — without giving away margin by bundling it in for free.

ApexSites Team·7 min read

A website build is a one-time transaction. Hosting is the line item that turns a client into a recurring revenue source — often worth more over two years than the original build fee. Yet many new agencies bundle hosting in "for free" to sweeten the initial deal, which quietly gives away the most durable part of the business.

Treat hosting as a deliberate add-on, not a freebie

Every plan should offer hosting as a paid, optional add-on rather than something included by default. This matters for two reasons: it protects your margin (you're paying an underlying hosting cost regardless of whether the client pays you for it), and it gives clients a clear reason to stay on a monthly relationship with you instead of treating the build as a one-off purchase.

How to price it

A common, defensible structure is a flat monthly hosting fee per site — priced well above your underlying cost, since the client is paying for reliability and a single point of contact, not raw server costs. Many agencies price hosting at $29–$79/month per site depending on the vertical and how much ongoing support is bundled in (uptime monitoring, minor copy edits, seasonal promotions).

Presenting hosting as valuable, not mandatory

Frame hosting the way you would any premium add-on: "I can host and manage this for you for $49/month, so you never have to think about renewals, uptime, or updates — or you're welcome to host it yourself if you'd prefer." Most local-business owners have no interest in managing their own hosting and will take the path of least resistance, especially once you've established trust through the build itself.

Stacking hosting with a care plan

  • Uptime monitoring and basic security patching
  • A set number of monthly copy or image swaps
  • Seasonal promotional banners or a homepage refresh
  • Priority turnaround on any urgent request

Bundling these into the hosting fee (rather than billing separately) makes the monthly charge feel like an obvious yes rather than a negotiation.
